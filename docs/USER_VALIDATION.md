# User validation interview

Use this short interview before adding a new source, industry pack, or workflow. Record the user's exact words and keep customer names, credentials, and private tender documents out of the public repository.

## Five questions

1. Where did you discover OpenBid Intel?
2. Which sentence, screenshot, result, or feature made you investigate further?
3. How do you find and qualify opportunities today?
4. How much time is spent each week collecting, deduplicating, reading, and routing notices?
5. What is the smallest real test you would be willing to run: synthetic demo, sanitized export, profile-pack contribution, connector fixture, or commercial workflow discussion?

## Minimum workflow facts

Capture:

- industry and typical offering;
- geographic scope;
- input format and source type;
- approximate notices reviewed per week;
- current qualification criteria;
- current output: spreadsheet, CRM, email, dashboard, or chat notification;
- one success metric;
- one compliance or privacy constraint.

## Evidence ladder

Treat signals differently:

1. page view;
2. repository star;
3. specific question;
4. synthetic demo completed;
5. sanitized real export tested;
6. repeated weekly use;
7. external contribution;
8. commercial or deployment discussion.

Do not call a project validated because it received traffic alone. The strongest early evidence is a user who runs the workflow on permitted data and returns with a specific request.

## Suggested success metrics

- notices reviewed before and after filtering;
- percentage of top-ranked notices judged relevant;
- minutes required to reach a qualified shortlist;
- duplicate notices removed;
- qualified opportunities routed to a responsible owner;
- repeated use one week later.
